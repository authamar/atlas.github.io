✈️ Airport strategy — Fresno (FAT) is your best base, SFO works too: Fresno Yosemite International (FAT) is 65 miles from Yosemite Valley — about 1 hr 15 min drive through the foothills on Hwy 41. United, Delta, and Southwest all serve FAT. SFO and SJC are ~3.5 hrs from the valley but offer more nonstop options from most cities. Book a mid-size SUV — Hwy 41 and Big Oak Flat Road are fine, but you’ll want ground clearance if you plan to drive Tioga Road (D5). Critical: Yosemite Valley requires a timed entry permit (7am Pacific, 5 months in advance) for day visitors from May through October — book this before you book anything else at recreation.gov. Lodging guests at in-park hotels do not need a separate permit.
🗺️ Logic of the route: FAT arrival → drive Hwy 41 north into the park (D0) → two full Valley days covering west and east floor (D1–D2) → Glacier Point Road for the high overlook (D3) → Mariposa Grove giant sequoias and Wawona (D4) → Tioga Road high country: Olmsted Point, Tenaya Lake, Tuolumne Meadows (D5) → early morning farewell walk and drive to FAT (D6). No backtracking — each day radiates from the valley or moves logically through the park.

Arrival — fly into Fresno (FAT)

Fresno Yosemite International (FAT)
Smaller airport, easy baggage claim and car rental. United, Delta, American, Southwest all serve FAT. Mariposa Grove South Entrance — quick stop
The South Entrance on Hwy 41 puts you right next to Mariposa Grove. Valley View — first look at El Capitan
Coming in on Wawona Road, just before you enter the valley proper, pull off at Valley View. El Capitan fills the entire left frame. The Merced River curves below. This is your child’s first sight of a 3,000-foot granite wall — watch their face. Easy pull-off, 30-second walk to the viewpoint. No crowds here (everyone rushes past to Tunnel View).
Valley View — El Capitan and Merced River on arrival

🚗 Valley View → Yosemite Valley Lodge: 4 mi · 8 min
5:00 pm
🏨
Check in — Yosemite Valley Lodge or Curry Village
Yosemite Valley Lodge is the best choice with a toddler: motel-style rooms, the Village Grill across the street, and Lower Yosemite Fall is a 5-minute walk. Curry Village is more affordable with canvas tent-cabins and a pizza deck with an El Capitan view. Both book out 6-12 months ahead at travelyosemite.com. If in-park lodging is unavailable, El Portal (8 mi outside) and Mariposa (30 mi) are fallback options.
☕ rest / meal
⚠️ Book your timed entry permit immediately: Yosemite Valley requires advance day-use permits from late April through October. They open at 7am Pacific exactly 5 months before your visit date and sell out in minutes. Set an alarm and be ready on recreation.gov. In-park hotel guests are exempt from this permit for the days of their stay.
D1

Valley Floor West — Tunnel View, Bridalveil Fall, El Cap Meadow

The greatest hits · all flat or near-flat · perfect for toddlers

7:00 am
🌅
Tunnel View — Yosemite’s iconic panorama
Drive through the Wawona Tunnel and emerge at the most photographed viewpoint in Yosemite. El Capitan anchors the left, Half Dome sits dead center in the distance, Bridalveil Fall threads down the right cliff, and the valley floor stretches away below you in green. Go at 7am before tour buses arrive — parking is a large lot and nearly empty early. Your 3-year-old can stand at the wall and absorb the scale. Morning light on El Cap is golden and direct.
Tunnel View — El Capitan, Half Dome, and Bridalveil Fall in one frame
📷 photo spot🌄 overlook
🚗 Tunnel View → Bridalveil Fall: 1 mi · 3 min
8:00 am
🌊
Bridalveil Fall Trail — toddler’s first waterfall
A 0.5-mile round-trip paved trail leads to the base of Bridalveil Fall, a 620-foot ribbon of white water that sways in the breeze like a veil (hence the name). The trail has a gentle grade. Get to the end of the path and you’re misted by spray — your toddler will go absolutely wild. Slippery rocks near the base, so hold hands. Best flow May–June; still impressive in July–August. Parking at the dedicated lot (fills by 9am in summer).
Bridalveil Fall — 620 ft of mist and spray, 0.5 mi from the car
🚶 easy walk📷 photo spot
🚗 Bridalveil → El Capitan Meadow: 2 mi · 5 min
9:30 am
🧭
El Capitan Meadow — look straight up at 3,000 feet
Pull off at El Capitan Meadow and lie on your back in the grass. The sheer granite face of El Capitan towers overhead — the largest exposed granite monolith on Earth. With binoculars, spot climbers inching up the nose. Your toddler can run freely across the flat meadow. Deer graze here in the morning. Best photo: shoot El Cap reflected in a puddle after rain. In summer, bring a blanket and picnic breakfast here.
📷 photo spot🌄 overlook🐾 wildlife
🚗 El Cap Meadow → Cook’s Meadow: 3 mi · 6 min
11:00 am
🌿
Cook’s Meadow Loop — flat stroller loop around the valley heart
A flat 1-mile paved loop in the center of Yosemite Valley with views of Yosemite Falls, Sentinel Rock, and Royal Arches in every direction. This is one of the best toddler walks in the park — completely flat, no elevation, and surrounded by meadow. The Sentinel Bridge arm of the loop gives you the classic Half Dome reflection in the Merced River (Ansel Adams photographed it from here). Toddlers can walk the whole thing.
🚶 easy walk📷 photo spot
1:00 pm
🍕
Lunch — Village Grill Deck or Curry Village Pizza Deck
Village Grill has a shaded outdoor deck with counter service burgers, wraps, and kids’ meals. Curry Village Pizza Deck has the best view — you eat pizza on picnic tables while looking directly at Glacier Point 3,200 feet above. Both are toddler-friendly. Nap time: head back to the lodge 1–3pm for the toddler’s nap, then return for late afternoon golden hour.
☕ rest / meal
5:00 pm
🌄
Valley View (return) — golden hour on El Capitan
Return to Valley View at 5pm. Late afternoon sun lights up El Capitan in warm orange–gold tones that look completely different from the morning. The Merced River catches the color. This 10-minute stop is one of the great free photography moments in the American West.
📷 photo spot🌄 overlook
🐻 Black bear activity: Yosemite has about 500 black bears in the park. Never leave food in your car (use bear boxes at campgrounds). If a bear approaches your car, honk the horn. Bears here are not dangerous unless provoked — but they are incredibly clever about getting food from vehicles.
D2

Valley Floor East — Yosemite Falls, Mirror Lake, Happy Isles

Waterfalls up close · lake reflections · Merced River play

7:30 am
🌊
Lower Yosemite Fall Trail — best toddler hike in the park
A 1-mile paved loop from Yosemite Valley Lodge leads to the base of Lower Yosemite Fall (320 ft), directly beneath the combined 2,425-ft Yosemite Falls — the tallest waterfall in North America. The trail is completely flat, paved, and stroller-accessible. In May–June, the spray soaks you; by August the falls slow to a trickle. Either way your toddler will stand in the mist and laugh. Go early to beat the 9am crowds. Walk the full loop past the footbridge for the best angle.
Yosemite Falls — 2,425 ft total, tallest waterfall in North America
🚶 easy walk📷 photo spot
9:30 am
🏙️
Yosemite Village — Visitor Center and Ansel Adams Gallery
Yosemite Village is the service hub of the park. Stop at the Yosemite Valley Visitor Center for ranger talks and the interactive exhibits (toddlers love the hands-on wildlife displays). Then walk 200 feet to the Ansel Adams Gallery — free entry, rotating exhibitions of his Yosemite photographs. The gift shop has excellent prints. Degnan’s Kitchen next door has great breakfast burritos and fresh coffee.
☕ rest / meal
🚌 Valley Shuttle Stop 17 → Mirror Lake trailhead: free YARTS shuttle
11:00 am
🧲
Mirror Lake Loop — Half Dome reflected in still water
Take the free Valley Shuttle to Stop 17 (no cars beyond this point). It’s a flat, paved 0.5-mile walk from the shuttle stop to Mirror Lake. In spring and early summer, the lake perfectly reflects Half Dome. By late summer it’s more of a sandy meadow (still beautiful). The 5-mile full loop is too long for a toddler — just walk to the lake (1 mile RT from shuttle stop) and let them play at the water’s edge. Tenaya Creek runs alongside — excellent for skipping rocks.
Mirror Lake — Half Dome reflected perfectly in spring and early summer
🚶 easy walk📷 photo spot🏖️ swimming
2:00 pm
🌊
Happy Isles — Merced River play area
Happy Isles is where the Merced River splits around two small islands near the valley’s east end. The water is shallow, clear, and cold — perfect for toddlers to wade and splash in July and August. The footbridge over the river gives excellent views of the canyon. This is also the trailhead for the Mist Trail to Vernal Fall — too difficult with a toddler, but you can walk the first 0.25 mile of flat pavement to see the river up close.
🏖️ swimming🐾 wildlife
🧔‍♀️ Mist Trail to Vernal Fall: You’ll see it mentioned everywhere. It’s one of the great hikes in America (2.4 mi RT to Vernal Fall, 7 mi to Nevada Fall). But with a 3-year-old, it is not safe — the granite steps are slippery and the crowds are chaotic. Save it for a future trip. The Happy Isles river area gives you the river experience safely.
D3

Glacier Point Road — Half Dome at eye level

The most dramatic overlook in the Sierra · 32 mi round trip from valley

8:00 am
🌄
Washburn Point — the hidden overlook before Glacier Point
One mile before Glacier Point on the Glacier Point Road, pull off at Washburn Point. Vastly fewer visitors than Glacier Point, and arguably the better view of the Clark Range and Nevada Fall in the distance. Half Dome sits to your left at the same elevation. You can see the entire Yosemite high country from here. A 2-minute walk from the parking area.
📷 photo spot🌄 overlook
🚗 Washburn Point → Glacier Point: 1 mi · 3 min
9:00 am
🏔️
Glacier Point — 3,214 feet above the valley floor
Glacier Point is the commanding high overlook of Yosemite Valley. Half Dome stands directly in front of you at nearly the same elevation. You can see Yosemite Falls, Vernal Fall, and Nevada Fall all at once. The valley floor — where you walked yesterday — is 3,200 feet directly below. There’s a railing at the overlook’s edge (hold your toddler firmly). The Glacier Point snack stand opens at 9am. Rangers give talks here throughout the day. Takes about 1.5 hours to enjoy properly.
Glacier Point — Half Dome and Nevada Fall, 3,214 ft above the valley
📷 photo spot🌄 overlook
🚗 Glacier Point → Valley → Swinging Bridge: 32 mi · 50 min
1:00 pm
🏖️
Swinging Bridge — Merced River swimming hole
The Merced River at Swinging Bridge (near Sentinel Beach) is the best family swimming spot in Yosemite Valley in summer. The water is clear and cold, the current slow, and the sandy beach is toddler-perfect. El Capitan looms directly above. Bring a picnic and a change of clothes. This is an afternoon that will live in family memory forever.
🏖️ swimming🐾 wildlife
🌐 Glacier Point Road opens late: The road to Glacier Point is typically closed until late May due to snow. Confirm it’s open at nps.gov/yose before your trip. It is sometimes also closed for controlled burns.
D4

Mariposa Grove & Wawona — the giant sequoias

Trees 3,000 years old · toddlers become tiny · historic Wawona Hotel

8:30 am
🌲
Mariposa Grove — 500 ancient giant sequoias
Mariposa Grove is the largest sequoia grove in Yosemite — 500 giant sequoias, including the 3,000-year-old Grizzly Giant (one of the oldest living things on Earth) and the California Tunnel Tree, which you can walk through. From the parking area, it’s 1.6 miles of paved trail to reach the Grizzly Giant and the Tunnel Tree. The grade is gentle and stroller-manageable (though a carrier might be easier). A free tram runs April–November if your toddler is spent. The scale destroys all sense of proportion — your child will look like a doll standing at the base of these trees.
Mariposa Grove — the Grizzly Giant and California Tunnel Tree
🚶 easy walk📷 photo spot🐾 wildlife
🚗 Mariposa Grove → Wawona Hotel: 2.5 mi · 6 min
12:00 pm
🏡
Wawona Hotel — lunch on the historic porch
The Wawona Hotel (built 1879) is one of the oldest resort hotels in California. The white Victorian-style main building has a wraparound porch with rocking chairs overlooking a croquet lawn. The dining room serves a good buffet lunch. This is a lovely, unhurried break with a toddler — grass lawn to run on, a small pool open in summer, and a nine-hole golf course that doubles as a wildlife meadow (deer and turkeys graze the fairways).
☕ rest / meal🐾 wildlife
2:00 pm
🛋️
Pioneer Yosemite History Center — covered bridge and horses
A short walk from Wawona Hotel, the Pioneer History Center has a collection of 19th-century buildings relocated from around the park: a covered bridge (children love running through it), a stagecoach barn, a cavalry post, a settler’s cabin. In summer, costumed rangers offer living history demonstrations and horse-drawn stagecoach rides that toddlers adore. Free entry. Budget 45 minutes.
🚶 easy walk🐾 wildlife
5:30 pm
🌿
Wawona Meadow Loop — deer at dusk
A flat 3.5-mile loop around the open Wawona Meadow begins right next to the hotel. You don’t need to do the full loop — just walk 15 minutes out and back along the meadow edge. Deer are almost guaranteed at this hour. The wide-open meadow with forest walls is peaceful after a full day. Then drive back to the valley (45 min) or stay overnight at Wawona Hotel.
🚶 easy walk🐾 wildlife
D5

Tioga Road — Olmsted Point, Tenaya Lake, Tuolumne Meadows

High Sierra at 9,900 ft · granite domes · alpine lake swimming · open Jun–Oct only

8:00 am
🧭
Olmsted Point — Half Dome from a completely different angle
Tioga Road (Hwy 120) climbs from the valley through forest and emerges into the open granite high country. Olmsted Point is a vast granite expanse with a direct view of the back of Half Dome — an angle almost no one sees. Glacially polished granite boulders are scattered everywhere. Toddlers can scramble on the smooth rock slabs (watch for sudden drop-offs at edges). Ground squirrels are everywhere and extremely tame (don’t feed them). The view extends back east to Tenaya Canyon. 10-minute stop minimum, 30-minute stop if your child loves rocks.
Olmsted Point — Half Dome’s rarely-seen backside and glacial granite
📷 photo spot🌄 overlook🐾 wildlife
🚗 Olmsted Point → Tenaya Lake: 2.5 mi · 5 min on Tioga Rd
9:30 am
🏖️
Tenaya Lake — the perfect alpine swimming lake
Tenaya Lake sits at 8,150 feet elevation surrounded by domes of bare white granite. The lake has a white sandy beach on its west end (large parking area) — wade in, the water is clear and cold (58–65°F in summer). Shallow for 20 feet from shore, ideal for small children. The domes above reflect in the surface. Even if you don’t swim, sit on the beach and eat the lunch you packed. This is the high Sierra at its most accessible and dramatic. Budget 1.5–2 hours here.
Tenaya Lake — granite domes, white sand beach, cold clear water
🏖️ swimming📷 photo spot🚶 easy walk
🚗 Tenaya Lake → Tuolumne Meadows: 8 mi · 12 min
12:30 pm
🌿
Tuolumne Meadows — the high Sierra’s great open room
At 8,600 feet, Tuolumne Meadows is the largest subalpine meadow in the Sierra Nevada — a vast green carpet threaded by the Tuolumne River, ringed by domes and peaks. Park at the main lot and walk the flat meadow trail (0.5–1 mile, completely flat). The Tuolumne Meadows Grill serves burgers and soft serve ice cream — eat on the porch watching the meadow. This is the start of many great High Sierra backpacking routes, but for families, just absorbing the scale and letting the toddler run through the grass is the whole experience. Marmots and black bears are often spotted near the edges.
🚶 easy walk🐾 wildlife📷 photo spot
3:00 pm
🧭
Dana Meadows viewpoint — drive-by of Mt. Dana and Ellery Lake
Continue east 5 miles past Tuolumne on Tioga Road to see Mt. Dana (13,057 ft) and the brilliant blue of Ellery Lake just outside the east entrance. Even if you just slow down and look, these views are extraordinary — Sierra peaks at eye level. Then turn around and drive back west. The late afternoon light on the granite domes as you return toward the valley is spectacular.
🌄 overlook📷 photo spot
❄️ Tioga Road is closed from November to late May due to snow. Check nps.gov/yose conditions before your trip. If it’s not open, substitute this day with a second Glacier Point visit or the Wawona area. Altitude (8,600 ft): toddlers adjust quickly, but drink extra water and watch for signs of altitude sickness (headache, nausea). Ascend slowly.
D6

Departure day — morning farewell, drive to FAT

Early valley quiet · Merced River walk · 1 hr 15 min to Fresno

6:30 am
🌅
Cook’s Meadow — sunrise farewell
Get up early and walk to Cook’s Meadow one last time. The valley at 6:30am is almost completely still. The light catches the top of Half Dome pink-orange before it touches anything else. No shuttle buses yet, no crowds. Your toddler may still be half-asleep in a carrier — that’s fine. This is one of those quiet moments that sticks.

☕
Breakfast — Village Grill or Degnan’s
Both open by 8am. Pack up, load the car, eat. Check out by 11am. Buy a Yosemite memento at the Valley Visitor Center gift shop before you leave — 100% of proceeds go to park programs.

🌄
Valley View — one last look
On your way out of the park via Hwy 41, stop at Valley View one final time. El Capitan glows in morning light. The Merced curves below. You’ve spent six days walking beneath these walls and now you’re leaving them behind. Your toddler will probably say “I want to stay.” They’re right.
📷 photo spot
🚗 Valley View → Fresno FAT Airport: 68 mi · 1 hr 15 min on Hwy 41 south
11:00 am
✈️
Fresno Airport (FAT) — return rental car
FAT is a small, easy airport — drop rental car, quick security, and you’re done. Book your return flights no earlier than 1:30pm to give yourself time. If you have a later flight, consider stopping in Fresno at the excellent Forestiere Underground Gardens (Baldasare Forestiere’s hand-carved subterranean home — strange and wonderful, and toddlers love the underground tunnels).

What to pack for a Yosemite trip with a toddler

🧴
Hiking carrier / backpack
Essential for Mariposa Grove and any uneven terrain. Toddlers tire quickly.
☀️
SPF 50 sunscreen
Valley reflects UV. High elevation (Tioga Road) intensifies exposure significantly.
👟
Water sandals + trail shoes
Merced River wading and trail walking both happen in the same day.
🎂
Dry snacks for the car
Trailheads often far from food. Cliff bars, fruit pouches, and cheese sticks.
🔭
Binoculars
Spot climbers on El Capitan and wildlife in meadows. Kids love their own pair.
🩹
First aid + blister kit
Granite edges are sharp. Pack band-aids and antiseptic wipes.
🍧
Layers / fleece
Valley evenings cool quickly. Tioga Road can be 40°F even in August.
💧
Reusable water bottles
Hydration stations throughout the park. Altitude increases fluid loss.
📱
Downloaded offline maps
Cell signal is poor in the valley and nonexistent on Tioga Road.
✅ Before you go — booking checklist
  • ☐ Yosemite timed entry permit (recreation.gov — 7am Pacific, 5 months out)
  • ☐ In-park lodging (travelyosemite.com — 6–12 months out)
  • ☐ Rental car with child car seat (one-way not needed — fly FAT both ways)
  • ☐ America the Beautiful Annual Pass ($80 — covers $35 Yosemite fee + any other park)
  • ☐ Verify Glacier Point Road and Tioga Road are open (nps.gov/yose)
  • ☐ Check bear canister rules if camping — required in wilderness, recommended in valley
